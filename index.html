What are the requirements to be designated as a CPA?

Requirements vary from state to state. Kentucky state law sets forth the following requirements for an individual to be licensed as a Certified Public Accountant:

  • Is of good moral character.
  • Has a bachelors or masters degree conferred by a college or university recognized by the state board of accountancy with a major or concentration program in accounting or its equivalent.
  • Has one year working experience in accounting or auditing.
  • Passes a state board-approved examination in accounting, auditing, and other related subjects.

What services does a CPA provide?

Many individuals traditionally associate the services of a CPA with businesses. Today, many individuals also rely upon the professional services of a Certified Public accountant. Just a few of the many services offered for individuals include:

  • Suggesting tax strategies.
  • Preparing tax returns.
  • Minimizing tax liability
  • Representation before taxing authorities.
  • Developing a personal financial plan.
  • Creating a budget.
  • Planning for retirement.
  • Developing an estate plan.
  • Assessing insurance needs.
  • Advising on divorce settlements.
  • Devising savings and investment strategies.
  • Helping to build college funds.

How do CPAs base their fees?

Fees are based upon the amount of time that is required to complete the service that you request. This time can be affected by the complexity and level of expertise required to complete the task. We welcome your inquiries as to estimated costs.
